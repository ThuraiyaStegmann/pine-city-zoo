LION

The lion (Panthera leo) is a species in the family Felidae and a member of the genus Panthera. It is a large cat of the subfamily Felinae that is native to Africa and India. The lion is the second-largest living cat after the tiger, and the only member of the genus Panthera to live in social groups.

Lions are apex predators, primarily preying on ungulates such as zebras and antelopes. They are territorial and generally social animals; they mark their territory with scent marks, scrape marks, and vocalisations.

Lions are known for their majestic manes, which vary in color and size among individuals. They are also known for their powerful roars, which can be heard from miles away.
